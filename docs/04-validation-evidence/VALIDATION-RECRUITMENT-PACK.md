# Fuel Path Recruitment Pack

Last updated: 19 June 2026, Australia/Sydney.

## Purpose

Move Goal 1 from technically credible to customer-proven with 7 real validation sessions. Synthetic dry runs and internal checks do not count.

## Recruit Targets

| Session | Segment | Must include |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Repeat commuter | Drives the same metro route at least 3 days per week. |
| 2 | Family or school-run driver | Price-sensitive, time-constrained, makes routine local trips. |
| 3 | Gig or delivery driver | Fuels often and makes route decisions during work. |
| 4 | Tradie or service worker | Drives between jobs and cares about convenience and uptime. |
| 5 | Small fleet buyer | Owner, operator or office manager exposed to fuel costs. |
| 6 | Regional or road-trip planner | Thinks about range, sparse stations and route safety. |
| 7 | Manual price shopper | Uses FuelCheck, Petrol Spy or maps but dislikes effort. |

## Screener

Use these before booking:

```text
1. How many days per week do you drive?
2. How often do you buy fuel?
3. What do you currently use to decide where or when to fuel?
4. Do you have a regular commute, work route, delivery route or regional route?
5. What would make you reject a cheaper fuel stop?
6. Are you comfortable doing a 30 minute product feedback session over video or in person?
```

Accept if they have a real fuel decision pattern. Reject if they rarely drive, cannot describe a recent fuel decision, or are only offering general app opinions.

## Invite

```text
Hi,

I am testing Fuel Path, a route-based fuel decision app. It tries to answer whether you should fill now, wait, or stop somewhere on your route based on price, detour, freshness and fuel type.

I am looking for blunt feedback from real drivers. The session takes about 30 minutes. I am testing the product, not you. There is no need to prepare.

Would you be open to a short session this week?

Thanks,
Leo
```

## Consent And Boundaries

Say this at the start:

```text
This is an internal validation prototype, not a public fuel-price service. I may take notes on what you say and do, but I will not record payment details, account credentials, home address, exact saved route labels or other private information. You can stop at any time.
```

Do not capture:

- exact home or work addresses
- registration numbers
- account credentials
- precise saved-route labels tied to identity
- screenshots containing private notifications or contacts

## Evidence Gate

Goal 1 can only be marked proven when:

- at least 7 real sessions are completed
- at least 5 participants understand net saving without heavy explanation, recorded as `understoodNetSavingWithoutExplanation`
- at least 4 would change a fuel decision or enable a useful route alert
- the median minimum worthwhile saving is recorded
- the main trust objections are specific and fixable
- all quotes and scores are captured in `VALIDATION-SESSION-WORKBOOK.md`

If these are not met, treat the product direction as promising but unproven.
