# Map UX And Competitor Functionality Notes

Last updated: 13 June 2026, Australia/Sydney

## What Leading Fuel Apps Emphasise

### PetrolSpy

- Map-first comparison for real-time fuel prices.
- Search near current location or by postcode/suburb.
- Fuel type selection.
- Price cycle graphs for major cities.
- User-submitted price updates and report-price flow.
- Notifications for best time to buy unleaded fuel.
- Coverage messaging includes FuelCheck NSW and FuelWatch WA feeds.

### NSW FuelCheck

- Real-time NSW and ACT fuel prices.
- Favourite stations and favourite addresses.
- Price alerts for favourite stations or threshold prices.
- My Trip to find cheapest fuel on a route.
- Report price mismatch.
- Trends for price cycle, cheapest day, price range and fuel-type comparison.

### Wider Best Practice

- Price pins must identify both station brand and price.
- Map and list should be linked by a shared selected state.
- Pins should use rank numbers when a recommendation/list rank exists.
- Station detail should expose source, freshness, fuel type, other available prices and action buttons.
- Core actions should be close to the selected station: navigate, favourite, alert, report/update price.
- Detailed explanation should be progressively disclosed rather than always pushing the map down.

## Applied To Fuel Path Demo

- Route map pins now show brand badge, rank and price.
- Near Me pins show brand badge and price.
- Ranked route cards share the same rank numbers as map pins.
- Selected station card added with pump price, user price, other fuels, freshness, status and actions.
- Route list can be sorted by smart score, price, saving or detour.
- The recommendation card is compact, with detailed reasoning behind disclosure.

## Product Gap To Keep

Fuel Path should not merely copy PetrolSpy or FuelCheck. The differentiated job remains:

> For this drive, with your vehicle, discounts and route tolerance, what is the best fuel decision?

The map should provide trust and control, while the ranking engine provides the product edge.
